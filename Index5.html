24 April 2024: A peek into history

We didn't get to enjoy breakfast at the hotel because some of us were scheduled for the three and a half hour Auschwitz-Birkenau guided tour at half past eight, and the rest of us at half past nine. So we got breakfast to go and ate on the bus, which was a two hour bus ride.I was among those who were scheduled for the eight-thirty tour, so not long after arriving, we went in. We had all heard about this dreadful place and how much suffering it caused both directly and indirectly to a number of people that, to this day, we can only approximate. We heard heartbreaking stories, like how prisoners called the places "canadas" because they were promised riches similar to what Canada had at the time, or how they were made to mark everything they left at the entrance to give them false hope of return, with a final false promise that "work will set you free" ("Arbeit macht frei" in German).

After visiting the two Auschwitz-Birkenau Nazi camps, we went to a nearby shopping mall where we had lunch and did some shopping.On the way back home, we had time to think about the horrors that happened, which made us more aware of how we behave towards those around us and the impact our actions have on both the long and short term.
